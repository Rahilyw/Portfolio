# PRODUCT.md

## What this is

Personal portfolio of Rahil Wijeyesekera, a third-year Computer Science student
at the University of Victoria (AI agents, systems tools, web apps). The site is
a playable pixel-art surf world: the landing page is an animated 8-bit ocean
with clickable islands, and every sub-page is an island in that same game
world.

## Register

register: brand

Design IS the product here. A visitor's first impression ("how was this
made?") is the pitch.

## Users

- Recruiters and hiring managers skimming for signal (projects, skills,
  experience) who should leave remembering "the pixel ocean portfolio."
- Fellow developers and hackathon teammates poking around for fun.
- Both arrive on laptop or phone, usually in daylight, for 1–5 minutes.

## Brand voice

Playful, personal, handcrafted, confident. An indie surf-adventure game, not a
corporate template. Three physical-object words: **arcade cabinet, waxed
surfboard, tide-pool**.

## Strategic principles

1. **One world, no register breaks.** Every page lives inside the same 8-bit
   ocean game: game HUD chrome, quest logs, character screens, RPG dialog
   boxes. No page falls back to "normal website."
2. **Content stays professional.** The framing is a game; the facts (projects,
   dates, skills) stay accurate and scannable. A recruiter must never have to
   fight the theme to find information.
3. **Motion = tide.** Ambient, slow, looping (canvas waves, bobbing, drifting
   clouds, day/night cycle). Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` everywhere.
4. **Chunky feedback.** Every interactive element presses like an arcade
   button: hard shadows, 2–4px displacement, instant steps() transitions.

## Anti-references

- Corporate SaaS portfolio templates (hero, three feature cards, testimonial).
- Editorial-magazine aesthetics (the previous sub-page design; retired).
- Smooth glassmorphism, soft gradients, rounded-2xl cards. This world is
  square-cornered and hard-shadowed.
